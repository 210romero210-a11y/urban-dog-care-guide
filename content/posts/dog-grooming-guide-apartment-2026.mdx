## Brushing Your Dog

Regular brushing is the foundation of grooming.

### How Often to Brush

| Coat Type | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| Short smooth | Weekly |
| Medium | 2-3 times weekly |
| Long/double coat | Daily |
| Curly/wire | Every 4-6 weeks |

### Brushing Techniques

**For short coats:**
1. Use bristle brush in direction of growth
2. Short strokes覆盖 entire body
3. Check for debris or mats
4. Finish with soft cloth for shine

**For long coats:**
1. Start with misting to prevent breakage
2. Use slicker brush in sections
3. Work from skin outward
4. Remove any mats carefully
5. Finish with comb

**For double coats:**
1. Use undercoat rake to remove loose undercoat
2. Follow with slicker for topcoat
3. Never shave double coats
4. Expect heavy shedding seasons

## Bathing Your Dog

Dogs don't need frequent baths, but when they do, here's how:

### How Often to Bathe

- Most dogs: Every 4-8 weeks
- Dogs with skin issues: Per vet guidance
- Oily breeds: May need more frequent
- Water-loving breeds: As needed

### Bathtime Steps

**Preparation:**
1. Brush out any mats first
2. Use lukewarm water
3. Have treats ready
4. Place non-slip mat in tub

**Washing:**
1. Wet coat thoroughly
2. Apply shampoo, avoiding eyes and ears
3. Massage into coat
4. Rinse completely — soap left causes irritation!

**Drying:**
1. Squeeze excess water
2. Use absorbent towels
3. Keep dog warm until fully dry
4. Never use human hairdryers on high

### Apartment Bathing Tips

For small dogs or apartments without tubs:

- Use kitchen sink for small breeds
- Showers work well with detachable heads
- Keep sessions quick and positive
- Have absorbent towels ready
- Consider dry shampoo between baths

## Nail Trimming

Overgrown nails cause pain and health issues.

### How to Trim

**Step 1: familiarize**
1. Let dog sniff clippers
2. Practice holding paw without trimming
3. Give treats for cooperation

**Step 2: visual preparation**
1. Identify the quick (pink area in light nails)
2. For dark nails, trim small amounts
3. Look for black dot as guide (quick is near)

**Step 3: trim carefully**
1. Hold paw firmly but gently
2. Cut at 45-degree angle
3. Trim small amounts
4. Have styptic powder ready just in case

### When to Trim

- Every 3-4 weeks
- If you hear clicking on floors
- When nails touch the ground when standing

### Nail Grinding Alternative

Dremel tools grind nails smoothly:

- Less risk of cutting quick
- Smoother edges
- Many dogs tolerate better
- Takes more time

## Ear Cleaning

Regular ear care prevents infections.

### Signs of Ear Issues

- Head shaking
- Scratching at ears
- Bad odor
- Red or swollen ears
- Discharge

### How to Clean

1. Use vet-approved ear cleaner
2. Apply drops to ear canal
3. Massage base of ear
4. Let dog shake
5. Wipe outer ear clean
6. Never use cotton swabs inside

## Special Situations

### Apartment Grooming Challenges

**Limited space:**
- Groom in bathroom or kitchen
- Use drop cloth for easy cleanup
- Consider outdoor grooming in nice weather

**Noise-sensitive dogs:**
- Introduce tools gradually
- Keep grooming sessions short
- Use treats throughout
- Consider professional groomer for stressful tasks

### Professional Grooming

When to see a professional:

- Breed-specific haircuts
- Severely matted coat
- Aggressive or anxious dogs
- Special handling needs

## Eye and Face Care

### Eye Cleaning

1. Use damp cloth around eyes
2. Gentle wiping outward
3. Check for redness or discharge
4. Trim hair around eyes if needed

### Face Trimming

- Keep hair short around eyes
- Clean tear stains regularly
- Trim muzzle hair for cleanliness
- Use blunt-nosed scissors near eyes

## Post-Grooming Health Checks

While grooming, check for:

- Lumps or bumps
- Hot spots
- Parasites (fleas, ticks)
- Skin irritation
- Unusual odors

## Senior Dog Grooming

Senior dogs need special consideration:

- Gentler handling
- Shorter sessions
- Warm water for baths
- Orthopedic mat in tub
- Frequent but gentle brushing

## Puppy Grooming

Start early for lifetime of cooperation:

- Handle paws, ears, mouth from puppyhood
- Use treats and praise
- Keep sessions short and fun
- Build up to full grooming
